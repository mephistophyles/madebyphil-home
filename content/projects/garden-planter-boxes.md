A set of four raised cedar planter boxes for our backyard vegetable garden, designed for durability and ease of maintenance.

## Design Requirements

- Raised height for easier access (no bending)
- Cedar construction for rot resistance
- Dimensions that work with standard lumber
- Bottom drainage with weed barrier

## Dimensions

Each planter is 4' x 2' x 18" tall, sized to use standard 8' cedar boards efficiently with minimal waste.

## Construction

The build was straightforward—butt joints reinforced with exterior screws, with a 1x4 cap rail for a finished look. I lined the inside with landscape fabric to prevent soil from washing out through the gaps while still allowing drainage.

## Materials List (per box)

- 6x 1x8x8 cedar boards
- 2x 4x4x8 cedar posts
- 1x 2x6x8 cedar (cap rail)
- Exterior wood screws
- Landscape fabric

## Results

The planters have been holding up well through their first season. The raised height makes a huge difference for daily watering and harvesting. Already planning to build two more for next year.
