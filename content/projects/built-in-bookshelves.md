A floor-to-ceiling built-in bookshelf system for our living room, designed to maximize storage while maintaining a clean, minimal aesthetic.

## Design Goals

- Floor-to-ceiling coverage to maximize vertical space
- Adjustable shelves for flexibility
- Integrated cable management for electronics
- Paint-grade finish to match existing trim

## Materials

- 3/4" MDF for the main structure
- 1x2 poplar for face frames
- Adjustable shelf pins
- Semi-gloss paint to match trim

## Build Process

The project started with careful measurements of the wall, accounting for the slight bow that older houses tend to have. I built the unit in three sections in the garage, then assembled everything in place.

The trickiest part was getting the face frames level and square when the walls weren't. I ended up scribing the side panels to follow the wall contours while keeping the front edges perfectly plumb.

## Lessons Learned

Plan for imperfection. Old houses are never square, and fighting that reality makes everything harder. It's better to design for adjustment than to expect precision.
