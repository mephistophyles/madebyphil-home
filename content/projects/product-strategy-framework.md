A practical framework for prioritizing product features that I developed while leading product at a startup.

## The Problem

Every product team faces the same challenge: too many good ideas, not enough time. Traditional prioritization methods like RICE scoring felt too mechanical and didn't capture the nuances of early-stage products.

## The Framework

I developed a three-lens approach:

### 1. Strategic Fit

Does this feature align with where we're trying to go? This sounds obvious, but it's easy to get distracted by features that would be "nice to have" but don't advance the core vision.

### 2. Leverage

Will this feature unlock other opportunities? Some features are enablers that make future features easier or possible. These often deserve higher priority than their immediate impact suggests.

### 3. Learning Value

What will we learn by building this? In early-stage products, learning is often more valuable than shipping. Features that validate or invalidate key assumptions should be prioritized.

## Application

For each feature candidate, score it 1-5 on each lens, then use a weighted sum based on your current stage. Early-stage products weight learning higher; growth-stage products weight leverage higher.

## Results

This framework helped us reduce our roadmap from 40+ items to a focused list of 8-10, with clear reasoning for each decision. More importantly, it gave us a shared language for discussing priorities.
