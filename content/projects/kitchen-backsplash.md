A classic white subway tile backsplash installation, completed over a weekend.

## Planning

The kitchen had painted drywall behind the stove and sink—functional but boring. Subway tile seemed like the right choice: timeless, affordable, and forgiving for a first-time tile project.

## Materials

- 3x6 white ceramic subway tile
- White unsanded grout
- Mastic adhesive
- Tile spacers (1/16")
- Wet saw rental

## The Process

### Day 1: Prep and First Rows

Prepping the walls took longer than expected—cleaning, marking level lines, and planning the layout to minimize cuts. I started from the center and worked outward to keep the pattern symmetrical.

The first row above the counter was the trickiest since there was nothing to support the tiles while the mastic set. I used painter's tape to hold them in place overnight.

### Day 2: Upper Rows and Grouting

The upper rows went faster once I had a base to build on. Cutting tiles for outlets and edges was tedious but the wet saw made clean cuts. Grouting was messy but satisfying—wiping away the haze to reveal the finished pattern.

## Lessons Learned

- Buy 15% more tile than you need (I ended up using 12% extra)
- Don't skip the level line; trust the line, not your eye
- Wipe grout haze sooner rather than later
