## The Problem

Our oven had these frail knobs, that would keep breaking off. At one point we were swapping them out to turn on different burners. Why didn't we order new ones? Because the OEMs were an outrageous ~$60 and the replacements we could find on Amazon didn't cover our model oven. 

## Design

I started by validating that the oven knob's back end fit on our oven and could easily twist the handle. This meant a bit of back and forth with the caliper and printer. Eventually I got a nice tight fit (in Bambu green). Then I started iterating on the design itself. I used Fusion360 to make the knob roughly similar to the original ones. Eventually opting for a slightly cleaner version that you see in the main image. 

## Iteration

I ended up using PETG, since it had the right balance between thermal and mechanical properties, and felt right. I did a few tests in PA and PET-CF, but the surface was rough and didn't quite feel right with our interior. So we went back to a matte grey. The original green was PLA and frankly would probably have been fine. I will upload the final file to makerspace at some point.
