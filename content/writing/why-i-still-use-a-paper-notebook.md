I work in software. I have access to every note-taking app ever created. And yet, my most important thinking happens in a $3 paper notebook.

## The Limitations Are the Point

Paper has obvious limitations:
- No search
- No sync
- No backups
- No formatting
- No links

But these limitations force a different kind of thinking. When you can't link to everything, you have to synthesize. When you can't search, you have to organize. When you can't format, you have to be clear.

## The Tactile Advantage

There's something about the physical act of writing that engages my brain differently. Studies back this up—handwriting activates more areas of the brain than typing.

But I don't need studies. I just know that when I'm stuck on a problem, 10 minutes with a notebook and pen gets me further than an hour of staring at a screen.

## What Goes in the Notebook

Not everything. The notebook is for:

- **Early-stage thinking** — Before ideas are ready for digital systems
- **Problem solving** — Working through complex issues with diagrams and lists
- **Meeting notes** — Capturing what matters, not transcribing everything
- **Daily planning** — What matters today, written by hand

Everything else goes digital. Once ideas are formed, they graduate to proper systems.

## The Notebook System

I've tried fancy notebook systems. Bullet journals. Getting Things Done. Various indexing schemes.

What I actually do:
1. Date each page
2. Write whatever needs writing
3. Star important items
4. Review weekly, transfer anything that matters

That's it. No color coding. No complex notation. Just writing.

## Digital for Storage, Analog for Thinking

The distinction I've landed on: digital tools are for **storing and organizing** information. Analog tools are for **generating and processing** ideas.

My phone captures things I need to remember. My notebook helps me figure out what to think.

## The Right Tool for the Job

I'm not anti-technology. I love my digital tools and use them constantly.

But the best thinking tool isn't always the most advanced one. Sometimes it's a piece of paper and a pen.
