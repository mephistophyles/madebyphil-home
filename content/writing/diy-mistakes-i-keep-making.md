After years of DIY projects, you'd think I'd have learned these lessons. And yet, here we are.

## Not Measuring the Space (Again)

Every single time I build something for a specific location, I make the same mistake: I measure the space once, write down the numbers, and then somehow build something that doesn't quite fit.

The problem isn't the initial measurement. It's that I don't re-measure at critical points in the build. Walls aren't perfectly straight. Floors aren't perfectly level. That 36-inch opening? It's actually 35.75 inches on one side and 36.25 on the other.

**What I should do:** Measure at multiple points. Account for irregularities in the design. Do a dry fit before any finish work.

**What I actually do:** Curse at the wall for being wrong.

## Underestimating Finish Time

Sanding. Painting. Sanding again. More painting. Waiting for things to dry. These tasks always take longer than the actual construction.

I'll spend a weekend building something beautiful, then rush through the finish because I'm tired of looking at it. The result is always obvious—drips, uneven coverage, visible brush strokes.

**The fix:** Budget more time for finishing than building. Seriously, double or triple the estimate.

## Buying Exactly Enough Material

Lumber is expensive. I get it. So I calculate exactly what I need and buy exactly that amount.

Then I make a mistake on one cut. Or find a board with a defect I didn't notice at the store. Or realize I measured wrong (see above). Now I need to make another trip to the store, which costs time and gas, and often the new lumber doesn't quite match the old lumber.

**The rule I ignore:** Buy 15-20% more material than you need. Return what you don't use.

## Skipping the Test Fit

"I'm sure this will work. I measured it. Twice, even!"

Famous last words before discovering that two pieces don't align, a screw is in the wrong place, or the whole assembly is somehow 2 inches too tall.

Test fits catch problems when they're still easy to fix. But they feel like extra work, so I skip them. Every time.

## The "While I'm At It" Scope Creep

"I'm just going to replace this cabinet door."

Three weeks later: "...so that's why I'm retiling the entire bathroom."

One project reveals another problem. That problem connects to something else. Before you know it, a simple repair has become a major renovation.

**The discipline I lack:** Finish the original project first. Write down the other issues. Address them separately.

## Why I Keep Making These Mistakes

The honest answer? Impatience. I want to see results. The careful, methodical approach feels slow, so I take shortcuts that end up taking longer.

I'm working on it. Slowly. Without measuring first.
